## Welcome to AIML Course Hub

This comprehensive online platform is designed for students pursuing **M.Tech (AIML) and M.Tech (DSE)** programs at **BITS Pilani, Work Integrated Learning Programs**. The website provides detailed study materials, resources, and guidance for semester 1 courses.

## üìö Courses Covered

This platform currently hosts comprehensive materials for the following courses:

### 1. **Math Foundations for AI/ML**
Master the mathematical fundamentals required for AI and ML. Covers linear algebra, calculus, probability, statistics, and optimization - the mathematical backbone of machine learning.

**Code**: AIML ZC417 / DSECT ZC417  
**Credits**: 4 (2-0-2)  
**Relevance**: Essential for understanding ML algorithms

### 2. **Machine Learning Fundamentals**
Learn core ML concepts including supervised learning (regression, classification), unsupervised learning (clustering), model evaluation, feature engineering, and ensemble methods.

**Code**: AIML ZC420 / DSECT ZC420  
**Credits**: 4 (2-0-2)  
**Relevance**: Foundation for practical ML applications

### 3. **Deep Neural Networks**
Explore deep learning architectures from basic neural networks through CNNs, RNNs, attention mechanisms, and transformers. Understand modern AI techniques.

**Code**: AIML ZC421 / DSECT ZC421  
**Credits**: 4 (2-0-2)  
**Relevance**: Cutting-edge techniques for computer vision and NLP

### 4. **Introduction to Statistical Methods**
Develop statistical analysis skills through probability theory, distributions, hypothesis testing, time series forecasting, and statistical inference methods.

**Code**: AIML ZC418 / DSECT ZC418  
**Credits**: 4 (2-0.5-1.5)  
**Course Author**: Dr Y V K Ravi Kumar  
**Relevance**: Critical for data-driven decision making

## üéì Learning Recommendations

### Prerequisite Knowledge
- **Math Foundations**: Ideal starting point for everyone
- **Statistical Methods**: Basic math and probability knowledge helpful
- **Machine Learning**: Complete Math Foundations first (or have strong math background)
- **Deep Learning**: Complete or understand Machine Learning first

### Time Commitment
- Each course: 4 credit units = 128 hours total
- Classroom lectures: 2 units = 64 hours
- Tutorial/exercises: 0.5 unit = 16 hours
- Self-study/lab: 1.5 units = 48 hours

### Suggested Learning Path
**Path A (Foundations First)**
1. Math Foundations
2. Statistical Methods
3. Machine Learning
4. Deep Neural Networks

**Path B (Practical Focus)**
1. Machine Learning
2. Deep Neural Networks
3. Math Foundations (deeper understanding)
4. Statistical Methods

**Path C (Mixed Approach)**
1. Statistical Methods (foundation)
2. Math Foundations (theory)
3. Machine Learning (application)
4. Deep Learning (advanced)
